Competition Guidelines






Points to note:

  1. By participating in the debate competition, contestants acknowledge and agree that the competitions may be photographed and filmed. The Organiser reserves the right to use any photographs, videos, audios, and any other forms of media captured during the competitions in promotional materials, publications, and other communication materials of the EOC without further notification or any compensation to contestants. If a contestant does not wish to be photographed or filmed, please inform the staff of the competition upon arrival at the venue. The Organiser will respect your privacy and exclude the contestant from any individual photo and video capturing where possible.

  2. Information collected will be used solely for purposes of registration, communications, and related activities of the debate competition. All personal information collected will be kept confidential and accessible only by the EOC and authorised service contractors. Contestants have the right to request access to, correct, or delete their personal data.

  3. If the following situations occur on the day of the debate competition, all competitions on that day (including afternoon competitions) will be rescheduled:

  1. The Hong Kong Observatory is still enforcing Tropical Cyclone Warning Signal No. 8 two hours before the competition on that day or Tropical Cyclone Warning Signal No. 8 will remain in force;

  2. The black rainstorm warning is in effect two hours before the competition on that day; or

  3. The Government issues an announcement about extreme conditions owing to adverse weather.

The EOC and the service provider shall not be deemed liable for any losses, damages or costs incurred by the contestants under the above circumstances.

  1. After the match of the competition commences, it will be suspended if the following situations occur:

  1. The Hong Kong Observatory announces that Tropical Cyclone Warning Signal No. 8 or black rainstorm warning will be hoisted in the coming two hours; or

  2. The Government issues an announcement about extreme conditions owing to adverse weather.

The EOC and the service provider shall not be deemed liable for any losses, damages or costs incurred by the contestants under the above circumstances.

  1. The EOC and the service provider will not be responsible for any personal injury or damage to property experienced during the competition.

  2. For any disputes or controversies arising from this debate competition, the EOC and the service provider reserve the right to make the final decision.
